Visualizing Retention: Telecom Customer Churn Analysis

Project Objective

This project analyzes a dataset of 7,043 subscribers to identify the specific behavioral patterns that lead to customer turnover. The goal is to move from reactive customer service to a proactive retention strategy by identifying high-risk segments before they cancel.

Visual Discovery

The following charts identify the key indicators of churn based on the exploratory data analysis.

Baseline Churn Levels
This bar chart shows the distribution of churned vs. retained customers, establishing the baseline attrition rate for the dataset.

Senior Citizen & Partner Impact
These charts break down churn by demographic factors, showing how partner status and age categories influence loyalty.

The First-Year Risk
This visualization highlights that customers are most likely to leave within their first 12 months, with risk dropping significantly after the first year.

Feature Correlations
A heatmap showing how strongly different services (like tech support or streaming) are mathematically related to the churn target.

Spending vs. Tenure
This scatter plot visualizes the relationship between monthly charges and total spend, helping identify high-paying customers at risk.

Internet Service Type
This comparison reveals that Fiber Optic customers have a significantly higher churn rate compared to DSL users.

Strategic Recommendations

The analysis identified that month-to-month contracts and Fiber Optic service tiers are the primary drivers of attrition. We recommend launching targeted retention pilot programs for high-value customers within these specific risk segments.
