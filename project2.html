Predictive Analytics: Customer Churn in Telecommunications

Project Objective

This project addresses the economic challenge of customer turnover within the telecommunications sector. By utilizing predictive modeling on a dataset of 7,043 records, the goal was to identify behavioral signatures that precede a cancellation, allowing for proactive retention strategies rather than reactive responses.

Visualizing Churn Drivers

A significant portion of this project focused on exploratory data analysis to identify which factors—contract types, service tiers, or billing methods—most heavily influence a customer's decision to leave. The following visualizations highlight the key risk factors discovered.

Baseline Churn: Establishing the ratio of retained customers versus those who have exited the service.

Demographic Segmentation: Analyzing how different customer age groups and partner statuses impact retention.

The Tenure Factor: Visualizing the "danger zone" for new subscribers during the initial months of service.

Feature Correlation: Identifying the mathematical relationship between service features and churn probability.

Financial Impact: Examining the relationship between monthly charges and the likelihood of turnover.

Service Specifics: Pinpointing specific offerings, such as Fiber Optic service, that correlate with higher churn rates.

Methodology & Results

Beyond visual discovery, the project employed XGBoost and Random Forest models to calculate specific risk scores for every customer. The analysis identified that month-to-month contracts and high monthly charges are the primary drivers of attrition.

Strategic Recommendations

The primary recommendation is to launch targeted retention pilot programs for the top 10% highest-value, highest-risk customers. By offering value-add service credits rather than simple price discounts, the company can preserve revenue while increasing long-term customer loyalty.
