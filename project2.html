Visualizing Retention: Telecom Customer Churn Analysis

Project Objective

In the telecommunications industry, retaining an existing customer is significantly more cost-effective than acquiring a new one. This project analyzes a dataset of 7,043 subscribers to identify the specific behavioral patterns and service configurations that lead to customer turnover. The ultimate goal is to move from reactive customer service to a proactive retention strategy.

Visualizing the Drivers of Churn

Data visualization was the primary tool used to uncover "pain points" in the customer journey. By examining demographics, contract types, and billing monthly charges, several distinct risk signatures were identified.

Core Insights

The analysis revealed that contract type and monthly charges are the most significant predictors of churn. Specifically, customers on month-to-month contracts or those utilizing Fiber Optic services without additional support bundles showed a markedly higher probability of leaving. These insights allow for the creation of targeted "Value at Risk" segments for the marketing department.

Strategic Recommendations

The primary recommendation is to implement a pilot retention program focusing on the top 10% highest-value, highest-risk customers. By offering proactive value-add incentives (such as streaming credits or data upgrades) rather than reactive discounts, the company can improve loyalty while protecting profit margins.
