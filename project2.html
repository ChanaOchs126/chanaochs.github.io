Predictive Analytics: Customer Churn in Telecommunications

Project Objective

This project addresses the economic challenge of customer churn within the telecommunications industry[cite: 11]. Because acquiring new customers is significantly more expensive than retaining existing ones, the goal was to build a system that identifies high-risk customers before they cancel their service.

Visualizing the Churn Landscape

The following visualizations were developed to identify the specific behavioral signatures of customers likely to churn. By analyzing a dataset of 7,043 records, several key "pain points" were identified[cite: 11].

Tenure Risk: Identifying the critical window where new customers are most likely to leave[cite: 6].

Churn Overview: Visualizing the baseline ratio of retained vs. lost customers[cite: 9].

Contract Impact: How different billing cycles influence long-term retention[cite: 7].

Service Tiers: Identifying specific product bundles, such as Fiber Optic service, that correlate with higher risk[cite: 5].

Modeling & Results

Beyond the visualizations, the project utilized advanced machine learning models, including XGBoost and Random Forest, to deliver accurate probability scores for each customer[cite: 12].

  • Key Predictors: Month-to-month contracts and high monthly charges emerged as the top indicators of churn[cite: 12].
  • Actionable Insight: By segmenting customers by "Value at Risk," the marketing team can prioritize retention offers for high-value users[cite: 12].

Strategic Recommendations

The analysis suggests launching targeted pilot campaigns for the top 10% highest-risk customers[cite: 12]. By intervening with value-add offers rather than deep discounts, the company can protect its bottom line and increase overall customer lifetime value.
