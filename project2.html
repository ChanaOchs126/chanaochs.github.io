Visualizing Retention: Telecom Customer Churn Analysis

Project Objective

In the telecommunications industry, retaining an existing customer is significantly more cost-effective than acquiring a new one. This project analyzes a dataset of 7,043 subscribers to identify the specific behavioral patterns that lead to customer turnover. The goal is to move from reactive customer service to a proactive retention strategy.

Visual Discovery

The following charts identify the "pain points" where customers are most likely to leave the service.

Who is Leaving?
This bar chart shows the total number of customers we kept versus those who left. It gives us a baseline look at how big the churn problem actually is.

Demographics at a Glance
This breakdown shows how different groups (like seniors or families) stay with the company. It helps us see if certain age groups need more attention.

The "New Customer" Risk
This chart highlights that customers are most likely to leave in their first few months. If they stay past the first year, they are much more likely to remain loyal.

Hidden Connections
This "Heatmap" shows which features are linked. For example, it shows how month-to-month contracts are strongly tied to a higher risk of leaving.

The Price Point Problem
This dotted plot shows the relationship between monthly bills and total charges. It helps us spot if customers with high monthly bills are the ones dropping off.

Service Type Impact
This chart looks at different internet types. It reveals that customers with Fiber Optic service actually leave more often than those with DSL.

Final Insights & Recommendations

The analysis revealed that contract type and monthly charges are the most significant predictors of churn. The primary recommendation is to implement a pilot retention program focusing on high-value, high-risk customers by offering proactive incentives like streaming credits or data upgrades.
