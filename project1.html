Proactive Safety: Prioritizing NYC's Uncontrolled Intersections

The Goal

In support of NYC's Vision Zero initiative, my team and I wanted to change how the city handles traffic safety. Instead of waiting for accidents to happen before installing signals, we built a way to predict which intersections are dangerous before a crash ever occurs.

How I Approached the Data

I integrated several massive datasets, including historical crash records from 2022 through early 2026 and community maintenance requests. By looking at the "structural signature" of these locations, I could identify patterns that lead to high-risk environments.

  • The Model: I used a Random Forest Classifier to process these complex geographic patterns.
  • Accuracy: The model hit a 97% recall rate, meaning it is incredibly effective at catching historically dangerous sites that need immediate attention.

Real-World Results

We successfully identified the top 100 intersections that the DOT should audit immediately. Interestingly, our results showed that risk isn't spread evenly—over half of the high-priority sites were concentrated in the Bronx, followed closely by Staten Island.

My Recommendations

Based on this data, I recommend that field teams be dispatched to audit the top 100 locations for structural issues. By fast-tracking low-cost safety fixes for sites with high risk scores, the city can move from being reactive to truly proactive.
