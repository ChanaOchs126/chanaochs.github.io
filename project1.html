Data-Driven Safety Prioritization for NYC Intersections

Project Overview

This project utilizes a predictive model to identify high-risk "uncontrolled" intersections across New York City[cite: 10]. By analyzing geographic and structural signatures, the goal is to provide the Department of Transportation (DOT) with a proactive roadmap to intervene before accidents occur, supporting the city's Vision Zero initiative[cite: 8, 10, 80].

Key Insights & Process

  • The Problem: NYC infrastructure improvements are often reactive, with signals installed only after a location has a history of injuries[cite: 12, 13]. This project seeks to identify "latent risk" before incidents happen[cite: 15].
  • Data Integration: The analysis synthesized four major datasets: NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions (2022-2026), Traffic Signal Study Requests, Signal Condition reports (311 data), and a comprehensive inventory of existing signals[cite: 17, 18, 19, 20, 21].
  • The Model: We utilized a Random Forest Classifier composed of 200 decision trees[cite: 22]. The model achieved a Recall score of 0.97, ensuring that 97% of historically dangerous sites were correctly captured for prioritization[cite: 24, 25].
  • Major Findings: The model identified the top 100 highest-priority intersections for audit, with 55% of the risk concentrated in the Bronx and 32% in Staten Island[cite: 27, 29, 30].

Recommendation & Action

The primary recommendation is to dispatch field teams to audit these high-risk locations and fast-track low-cost interventions for any site with a Risk Score above 0.95[cite: 33, 34]. This allows the city to move from a reactive safety posture to a proactive one[cite: 42].
