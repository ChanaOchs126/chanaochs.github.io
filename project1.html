Proactive Safety: Prioritizing NYC's Uncontrolled Intersections

The Goal

In support of NYC's Vision Zero initiative, my team and I set out to transform how the city approaches traffic safety[cite: 8, 10, 80]. Instead of relying on reactive measures, we developed a predictive model to identify high-risk intersections before accidents occur, allowing for proactive infrastructure improvements[cite: 10].

Data-Driven Approach

I analyzed multiple datasets, including historical crash records from 2022 through March 2026 and community maintenance requests for traffic signals[cite: 17, 18, 19, 20]. By identifying the "structural signature" of these locations, I was able to pinpoint where future risks are highest[cite: 14, 15].

  • The Model: I utilized a Random Forest Classifier consisting of 200 decision trees to recognize complex geographic risk patterns[cite: 22].
  • Accuracy: The model achieved a 97% recall rate, ensuring that the vast majority of historically dangerous sites are prioritized for audit[cite: 24, 25].

Key Findings

Our analysis successfully identified the top 100 intersections requiring immediate attention[cite: 27]. The risk is heavily concentrated geographically, with 55% of priority locations found in the Bronx and 32% in Staten Island[cite: 29, 30].

Recommendations

Based on these findings, I recommend dispatching field teams to conduct structural audits at these 100 high-risk locations[cite: 33]. By fast-tracking low-cost interventions at sites with a high risk score, the city can take a meaningful step toward achieving Vision Zero[cite: 34, 43].
