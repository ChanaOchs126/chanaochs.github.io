Data-Driven Safety Prioritization for NYC Intersections

Executive Summary

This initiative leverages predictive analytics to identify high-risk intersections across New York City. By moving beyond reactive safety measures, this model provides the Department of Transportation (DOT) with a proactive framework to implement infrastructure changes before accidents occur, supporting the city's Vision Zero goals[cite: 10, 80].

Analysis & Methodology

  • The Challenge: Urban infrastructure improvements are traditionally reactive. This study identifies "latent risk" signatures at intersections that have not yet established a history of accidents[cite: 12, 13, 15].
  • Data Sources: The model integrates historical crash data (2022-2026), public traffic study requests, and infrastructure maintenance reports[cite: 17, 18, 19, 20, 21].
  • Machine Learning: A Random Forest Classifier was trained to recognize geographic risk patterns. The model achieved a Recall score of 0.97, effectively capturing nearly all high-priority sites[cite: 22, 24, 25].

Strategic Results

Our findings pinpointed the top 100 intersections requiring immediate audit. Geographic distribution shows that risk is heavily concentrated, with 55% of priority locations in the Bronx and 32% in Staten Island[cite: 27, 29, 30].

Recommendations

We recommend dispatching field teams for immediate structural audits at high-risk locations and fast-tracking low-cost safety interventions for sites with a Risk Score exceeding 0.95[cite: 33, 34].
