Proactive Safety: Prioritizing NYC's Uncontrolled Intersections

Project Objective

Aligned with New York City's Vision Zero initiative, this project focuses on transforming traffic safety from a reactive model to a proactive one. By identifying high-risk intersections through predictive analytics, the goal is to implement infrastructure changes before accidents occur, rather than responding only after a history of injuries has been established.

Methodology & Data Integration

The analysis synthesized four major datasets to identify latent risk patterns, including NYC Motor Vehicle Collisions (2022–2026), Traffic Signal Study Requests, and infrastructure maintenance records. By examining the geographic and structural signatures of these locations, the model identifies intersections with a high probability of future incidents.

  • Model Selection: A Random Forest Classifier with 200 decision trees was utilized to process complex spatial variables and infrastructure patterns.
  • Performance: The model prioritized Recall over simple accuracy, achieving a score of 0.97. This ensures that 97% of historically dangerous sites are correctly identified for safety audits.

Analysis Results

The predictive model pinpointed the 100 highest-priority intersections for immediate review. Data visualization indicates that risk is heavily concentrated in specific boroughs, with 55% of priority locations identified in the Bronx and 32% in Staten Island.

Strategic Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the primary recommendation is the immediate dispatch of field teams to audit the identified top 100 high-risk locations. Furthermore, low-cost safety interventions should be fast-tracked for any site returning a Risk Score above 0.95, effectively shifting the city's safety posture to a proactive footing.
